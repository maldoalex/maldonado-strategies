About Me

Leadership with Grace

  • Maldonado is an effective relationship builder and has an expansive network to decision makers to move your business initiatives forward.
  • Turning challenges into successes, Diana is an extraordinary Austin political and public figure and leads with grace, collaboration and commitment to bring desired results. Over the years, her success comes from listening to people’s needs and finding solutions for businesses & communities.
  • During her downtime, Diana enjoys doing destination marathon races, domestic and internationally and aiming to complete the six World Majors in the near future.

Accomplishments

  • First Latina elected to represent Williamson County in the Texas House of Representatives, authored/co-authored 88 bills and was named “Freshman of the Year” by her colleagues.
  • Secured nearly $16 million for education, $10 million for road expansion, and additional funds for renewable energy initiatives and walking trails in parks.
  • Appointed to State Affairs and Defense & Veterans Affairs Committees.
  • Executive role for the Mexican American Legislative Caucus
  • President & CEO at the Greater Austin Hispanic Chamber of Commerce and successfully led membership engagement, strategic policy initiatives and revenue diversification efforts.
  • Expanded advocacy and legislative efforts during a historic moment for Latinos in their advancement and representation as the largest demographic in the United States, as of mid-2021.
  • Financial advisor for one of the world’s largest investment firms, she guided individuals through financial planning strategies with an emphasis on women & wealth initiatives.
  • Round Rock ISD Board of Trustees and as president, oversaw a successful $349 million bond election while improving academic outcomes and the district was on the "best of" lists for Texas Monthly and Forbes.
  • Serves on multiple panels and discussions on business, policy and finance.
  • Two decade career at the Texas State Comptroller’s Office.
